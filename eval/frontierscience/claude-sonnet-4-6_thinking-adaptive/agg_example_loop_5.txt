Problem:

HEK293T cells were treated with 2.5 µM CPT, a DNA topoisomerase I inhibitor, after which the RNA for gene X was quantified with RT-PCR and agarose gel electrophoresis. The results of this reaction show a band 2 kb in size, even though computational analysis of the genomic sequence of gene X suggests a 2300 bp product. Next, the experiment was repeated, but this time the cells were also treated with 1 µM trichostatin A (TSA). This time, the resulting electrophoresis result shows a strong band at 2.3 kb, along with a faint band at 2000 bp. Based on these experiments, what process appears to be the main driver of gene X alternative splicing?

## Step-by-Step Analysis

### Step 1: Interpret the RT-PCR Results

| Condition | Band(s) Observed | Interpretation |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| CPT alone | 2.0 kb | ~300 bp shorter than predicted → a **~300 bp internal exon is skipped** |
| CPT + TSA | Strong 2.3 kb + faint 2.0 kb | Exon skipping largely **reversed**; full-length transcript predominates |

The computationally predicted 2.3 kb product represents the canonical, fully-spliced mRNA. CPT treatment yields only a 2.0 kb product, indicating a ~300 bp internal exon is excluded via alternative splicing.

---

### Step 2: Drug Mechanisms

**CPT (Camptothecin):** Traps the Topoisomerase I–DNA covalent cleavage complex, generating unresolved torsional stress ahead of elongating RNA Polymerase II (RNAPII). This activates the DNA Damage Response (DDR), which can **recruit HDAC complexes** to the affected gene locus, causing local histone deacetylation and chromatin compaction. Importantly, CPT does not directly inhibit HDACs.

**TSA (Trichostatin A):** Inhibits class I/II HDACs, preventing removal of acetyl groups from histone tails. This maintains **histone hyperacetylation** and promotes open, accessible chromatin. Critically, TSA does **not** restore Topoisomerase I activity — CPT's direct enzymatic effects persist.

---

### Step 3: The Decisive Logical Inference

The key observation is that **TSA largely reverses exon skipping even though CPT is still present and Topoisomerase I remains inhibited**. Since TSA acts exclusively on HDACs (not on Topo I), the rescue of exon inclusion cannot be due to restoration of Topo I function or resolution of torsional stress. The reversal must occur through an **HDAC-dependent chromatin pathway**, directly implicating histone deacetylation as the primary mechanism.

---

### Step 4: Mechanistic Chain of Events (Kinetic Coupling Model)

1. **CPT → Topo I inhibition → DDR activation → HDAC recruitment** at the gene X locus
2. **HDACs deacetylate histones → chromatin compaction** around the ~300 bp alternative exon
3. **Compact chromatin → accelerated RNAPII elongation** through the alternative exon — the spliceosome has insufficient time ("window of opportunity") to recognize the exon's weaker splice sites before RNAPII passes
4. **Result: exon skipping → 2.0 kb product**

With CPT + TSA:
- HDACs are inhibited → histones remain hyperacetylated → **open chromatin maintained**
- RNAPII elongation kinetics are restored → adequate co-transcriptional time for spliceosomal recognition
- **Result: exon inclusion dominates → strong 2.3 kb product** (faint 2.0 kb reflects residual minor contributions)

---

|-----------|--------------|-----------|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| CPT alone | Deacetylated (HDACs active) | Compact | Too fast → insufficient spliceosome window | Exon **skipping** (2.0 kb) |
| CPT + TSA | Hyperacetylated (HDACs blocked) | Open | Appropriately paced | Exon **inclusion** (2.3 kb) |

---

### Conclusion

CPT-induced Topoisomerase I inhibition activates the DDR, which recruits HDACs to the gene X locus. HDAC-mediated histone deacetylation leads to chromatin compaction around the ~300 bp alternative exon, accelerating RNAPII elongation such that co-transcriptional spliceosomal recognition of the exon's weaker splice sites fails (kinetic coupling model), producing the shorter 2.0 kb transcript. TSA, by inhibiting HDACs and restoring histone hyperacetylation and open chromatin, rescues exon inclusion, confirming that the main driver is **histone deacetylation (HDAC-mediated chromatin compaction)**.
